What is chus.css?

1st January 2024 / 2,504 views

A classless light solution to my CSS skill issues as a backend developer, enabling me to build visually appealing websites with a unique touch of personal design flair, or at least, I believe so.

Inspired on water.css, simple.css, skeleton and theminimalists.com.

Disclaimer: I am not a frontend developer. I dont know what I'm doing at all.

Layout and basics

The layout is based on a 600px width body that contains a main that expands his height to the top and bottom of the viewport. The idea is letting the lateral sides absolutely CLEAR, forcing me to craft a minimal beauty mobile first design.

Colors, spacing and typography are set to my personal taste after reviewing others CSS frameworks, like the ones mentioned above.

Extra classes

There are some extra classes I realized I needed while building interfaces.

.full

It applies a width of 100% to the element. Useful for buttons, tables or elements with flex display.

.center

My personal favourite way of centering elements. It applies display: flex, justify-content: center and align-items: center.

.notice

It applies a background color, a border and a padding indicating a notice.
